What is R?

To answer this question, just type "R wiki". And here is your answer "R is a programming language and software environment for statistical computing and graphics. The R language is widely used among statisticians for developing statistical software and data analysis."

Why R over Excel ?
  • R is free.
  • R is well-documented.
  • R runs (really well) on *nix as well as Windows and Mac OS.
  • R is open-source
  • R creates far better graphics than Excel and many more.
Basic Graphs in R -  The most exciting feature of R, is the ability to plot graphs. I always had to struggle in Excel to draw any graph but in R it was much more comfortable. Here are some simple steps to help you out with your first graph.
  1. Lets us assign a vector cars <- c(1, 3, 6, 4, 9)
  2. To plot a simple line graph  plot(cars, type="l"). 
where, type is what type of graph should be drawn. Example,  
  • "p" for points,
  • "l" for lines,
  • "b" for both, 
  • "o" for both overplotted,  
    3. We can assign color to graph by adding attribute col.  
         plot(cars, type="l", col="red") 
    
    4. If you want to display a text in the middle of the graph. Mention the x and the y co-ordinate with the      text to be displayed.
         text(3.0, 5.0, "Arpita")
    
     5. You don't want to display the numbering on Y - axis, attribute yaxt='n' is helpful.
          plot(cars, type="l", lty=2, col="red", yaxt='n')
     
     6. To remove the labels displayed on x and y axis.
         plot(cars, type="l", lty=2, col="red", ann=FALSE)
      7. To remove the border from y-axis
          plot(cars, type="l", lty=2, col="blue", bty='n',yaxt='n')


These are just few very simple graphs to make you comfortable with R and Graphs. We can implement fancy bar plots, dot plots, line charts, pie charts and scatter plots from R. These are few examples which I practiced during my course of learning. While going through my course of learning "Quick R" was very helpful.
